  • Application documents
    • For undergraduate applications, the required documents are:
    • Record of completed upper secondary (high school) education
    • Identification document.
    • Meeting the English requirement (6.5 IELTS , 72 TOEFL , 76 PTE)
    • As documents from this country are normally issued in English, you don't need to provide translations.
    • Eligibility Statement
    For masters applications, the required documents are:
    • A Bachelor's degree meets the general criteria. Please note that your program must be accredited by the Higher Education Relevance and Quality Agency (HERQA) at the time of your studies.
    • Reference Letters.
    • Motivation Letter.
    • Resume/ Proof of Work Experience.
  • The application process consists of the following steps:
    • Create an account on the Swedish University Admissions website: You will need to create an account on the Swedish University Admissions website in order to apply for university in Sweden.
    • Submit your application: Once you have created an account, you can start submitting your application. This will include things like your personal statement, transcripts, letters of recommendation, and English language test scores.
    • Pay the application fee: You will need to pay an application fee in order to apply for university in Sweden. The application fee is 900 SEK (around 90 EUR).
    • Your official transcript must be sent directly from your university to University Admissions in Sweden
    • Wait for a decision: Once you have submitted your application, you will need to wait for a decision from the university. The decision will be sent to you by email.
  • Financial Document Requirements
    • Tuition fee - 8,000 to 15,000 EUR
    • Living expense - 765 to 1,292 EUR

  • Scholarships
    • The Swedish Institute - The Swedish Institute, a government authority, receives funding each year for scholarships to international students. They have several programmes available.
    • Swedish Universities - Scholarships are available from the majority of Sweden's universities. You must apply directly to the individual university in which you're interested.
